YouTube was registered in 2005 by Steve Chen, Chad Hurley and Jawed Karim. The three men wanted YouTube to be a space where
ordinary people could share home videos for people to watch and enjoy. During the beta time period (May 2005) the site had
up to 30 000 visitors a day and by the time it officially launched (December 2005) the site had over two million visits a day.
It took merely one month for YouTube to have more than twenty-five million users and was producing 20 000 new videos a day and
by July of 2006 YouTube had a hundred million videos being watched every day. These numbers did not slow down. This immense
growth in users meant the owners of YouTube could not keep up with the hardware and broadband connections and could not afford
the upkeep of the site, thus they started looking for someone to buy the site. In November of 2006 Google bought YouTube for
1.65 billion U.S. dollars in stock. It is at this point that YouTube included the copyrighted content agreement, which allowed
youtubers to include certain copyrighted music in their videos, YouTube also removed tens of thousands of copyrighted videos
from the site to avoid copyright lawsuits. In 2008 Google struck a deal with MGM to play some of their videos on YouTube with
advertisements running alongside the videos.

YouTube has been a massive internet sensation and source of entertainment since it was created. This site sparked the era that
was slowly but surely making television obsolete seeing that it is a free site where users can watch exactly what they wanted
to with a seemingly endless well of videos to watch. YouTube also became a source of useful information since tutorial and
educational videos were starting to circle. This made it possible for anyone with internet access to learn almost anything.
YouTube has had its share of controversies over the years, which is expected from a public platform’ but it still remains
a massive part of internet culture.
