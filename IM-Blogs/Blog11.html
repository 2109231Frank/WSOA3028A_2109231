IMPLICIT BIAS AND SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT

So, I took an IAT (Implicit Association Test) on the implicit bias website. The test that I took involved sexual preference, the test entailed that I press buttons to sort words and figures into categories, like straight and gay or good and bad, they later on put gay and good in one category and straight and bad in the other and then switched the two later on.

I felt extremely uncomfortable categorizing bad and straight things together and also with gay and bad things, it felt really brain-washy in a sense. I understand that the tests are for educational purposes and should not be taking too seriously, but that being said it still made me uncomfortable to answer the questions in the way that they wanted me to. I do think it is important to understand your personal bias if you are going to design a website proper usability. We need to acknowledge our biases in order to counteract them so that we can create subjectively. If a web developer, for example, is biased with regards to politics he or she might design a website that is subconsciously leaned towards one pole of the political scale that would, in turn, make the site feel unfriendly or less inclusive to the users that are of a different political standpoint. This is why this specific user should figure out and understand his or her political bias in order to avoid funnelling their users and losing potential future users.

Even though this test made me uncomfortable and the results (that I shall keep private) did not exactly tell me anything that will help me improve my site: I believe that this is just the case because of my personal feelings, and I think that other tests would be more applicable to web development and to counteract my personal biases. I will conclude this post by saying that I believe that it is extremely important for web developers to figure out their biases, so that they can understand how to counteract this.
