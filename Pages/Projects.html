My Ocean

Tall waves crash on the beach seeming most intimidating
Yet the deeper I go the calmer it seems
Beneath the ocean’s angry eruptions, she is silent, she is dark…
she is elegant
Sitting on the soft sand under the water staring into the murky abyss
I find tranquillity
I find love
For I know the life that the ocean hides
I understand the respect she demands and deserves
When I step out from her ever pulling grasp
I long until I move into her again


Why I Wrote This

I was inspired to write this poem a few weeks ago when I came back from
my girlfriend's house. It is essentially a poem about how me and her met
three years ago at a party.

Older

My body hurts more
The past seems blurrier yet is understood better
The days of running around in the sun for hours are behind me
Time does not wait for us, so we just grow older without realising it

It is terrifying

But what lightens the load is finding someone to love forevermore
Someone whose name seems charming to the last letter
I do not feel old, for you fill me with a youthful euphoria
I am not scared anymore, because I will share growing old with you… every bit
And what we love about each other will like time, remain constant


Why I Wrote This

I am personally terrified of growing old for some reason and this poem
is inspired by the fact that my girlfriend silences this fear of mine.
Since me and her started dating I have become more and more comfortable
with the idea of growing old (specifically alongside her).

Your're Not Here Anymore

Sometimes I stare at your window
But you are not there, not anymore
But I shall wait uncomplainingly
Wait for this torture to end
And for you to come back home to me
For I miss you… and being without you is the lowest low


Why I Wrote This

When me and my girlfriend started dating each other we lived
on the same street, so we saw each other almost every day.
After a year or so she had to move because of work, and we
very soon started seeing each other every two months or so
if we were lucky. This poem was inspired by how much I miss
my girlfriend.
